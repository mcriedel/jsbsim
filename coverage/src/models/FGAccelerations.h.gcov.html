      56             : /** Handles the calculation of accelerations.
      57             : 
      58             :     - Calculate the angular accelerations
      59             :     - Calculate the translational accelerations
      60             : 
      61             :     This class is collecting all the forces and the moments acting on the body
      62             :     to calculate the corresponding accelerations according to Newton's second
      63             :     law. This is also where the friction forces related to the ground reactions
      64             :     are evaluated.
      65             : 
      66             :     JSBSim provides several ways to calculate the influence of the gravity on
      67             :     the vehicle. The different options can be selected via the following
      68             :     properties :
      69             :     @property simulation/gravity-model (read/write) Selects the gravity model.
      70             :               Two options are available : 0 (Standard gravity assuming the Earth
      71             :               is spherical) or 1 (WGS84 gravity taking the Earth oblateness into
      72             :               account). WGS84 gravity is the default.
      73             :     @property simulation/gravitational-torque (read/write) Enables/disables the
      74             :               calculations of the gravitational torque on the vehicle. This is
      75             :               mainly relevant for spacecrafts that are orbiting at low altitudes.
      76             :               Gravitational torque calculations are disabled by default.
      77             : 
      78             :     Special care is taken in the calculations to obtain maximum fidelity in
      79             :     JSBSim results. In FGAccelerations, this is obtained by avoiding as much as
      80             :     possible the transformations from one frame to another. As a consequence,
      81             :     the frames in which the accelerations are primarily evaluated are dictated
      82             :     by the frames in which FGPropagate resolves the equations of movement (the
      83             :     ECI frame for the translations and the body frame for the rotations).
      84             : 
      85             :     @see Mark Harris and Robert Lyle, "Spacecraft Gravitational Torques",
      86             :          NASA SP-8024, May 1969
      87             : 
      88             :     @author Jon S. Berndt, Mathias Froehlich, Bertrand Coconnier
      89             :   */
